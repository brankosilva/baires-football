FAQ

Common questions

Is it safe?+
Yes. You're always in a group with an experienced bilingual guide who has been to dozens of these matches. They handle all security checkpoints, entry, and the exit — you never have to figure anything out alone.
Do I need to speak Spanish?+
Not at all. Your bilingual guide handles everything and explains the history, the chants, and the context throughout the day.
What if I know nothing about football?+
No problem. This is a cultural experience first. Many guests have never watched a match before and still say it was the highlight of their whole trip.
How do I find out what's on?+
Write me and tell me your dates in Buenos Aires. Argentine football fixtures are confirmed 1–2 weeks in advance — I'll check what's available and send you the options.
What should I bring?+
National photo ID (not a screenshot), casual comfortable clothing, and some cash for extras. No bags, power banks, water bottles, or umbrellas — these get confiscated at the entrance.
